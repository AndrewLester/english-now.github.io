Partnering to Support International Residents of the Washington, D.C. Area & International Students

English Now!'s staff is comprised of educators who find great joy in serving international residents of the Washington, D.C., area as well as visiting international students. We strive to meet the specific language education needs of our students. At the same time, we aim to help our students thrive in the U.S. as they adjust to a new culture and community- a goal we share with other organizations that work with international families.

Shared Goals - Supporting International Residents and Their Families

Do you and your organization want to help internationals thrive during their time in the U.S.? English Now! works with many types of organizations to achieve this goal:

  • Schools that serve international learners;
  • Embassies;
  • International organizations, including their family organizations;
  • Non-profit organizations with missions that we share; and
  • Businesses with international staff.

We welcome the opportunity to work with your organization in providing support to international residents and short-term visitors.

Tools and Resources for You & Your Organization

We describe programs, tools and resources below that may be useful, and that may be part of our collaboration. Contact us if you have questions about any of the following - or better yet, please complete the webform to receive regular updates about public programs and additions to our community resources.

Programs on the U.S. Education System

  • Fall Workshops for Parents with Children in U.S. Schools - We will hold Fall Workshops for parents at our school in Bethesda. These public programs - Succeeding in U.S. Schools ; Understanding Your Child's First Weeks of School - are designed to answer the many questions international families have about U.S. schools. These workshops will be held on Friday, September 4; and Friday, October 2, from 10:00 until 11:30am. Please download the flyer here.
  • The presentation we deliver at this program is available for use for you with your community. (Download the Powerpoint presentation here.)
  • We also stock copies of an outstanding book by one of our partners, the Interchange Institute, called "Understanding American Schools: The Answers to Newcomers' Most Frequently Asked Questions". This is a great book for educators and for anyone who supports international families. See here for more information, or visit us and peruse a copy.
  • Fall Open House for Educators - Finally, we will hold a Fall Open House for educators and others who support international residents, on Saturday, September 26. Please complete the webform on the left for more information.

Programs on Success in Cross-Cultural Interactions

  • We will offer a very special program on success in cross-cultural training, led by Dr, Anne Copeland, the Founder and Executive Director of the Interchange Institute, on Tuesday, November 2.
  • Anne is a leading authority and noted speaker on cross-cultural issues, and we will be delighted to welcome members of the Washington, D.C.-area international community at the school for this program, and for Anne to share some of her insights. For more background on Anne, see here - and we suggest you consider subscribing to her e-newsletter as well!
  • This program is designed for educators but is open to representatives of other international organizations. Please let us know if you are interested in attending, by completing the webform and mentioning this program.

Programs on Parenting

  • One special program that is particularly helpful for newly arrived families is our "Parent Talk" conversation club, designed for mothers and their young children.
  • This program is offered seasonally, Fall through Spring, on Tuesday afternoons and Friday mornings. Please encourage mothers who may lack the support network that they have in their home country, to contact us for more information.

ENcompass: Information and Ideas for the International Community

  • We provide information online, as "ENcompass", that is designed to support international families' and students' transition to live in the U.S. Samples of articles include our recommendations - based on lots of experience - about the best things to do in the summer in and around Washington, D.C., and advice about the TOEFL test and applying for graduate schools. We invite you to read and share ENcompass.
  • Please also complete the web form on the left, to receive future ENcompass ideas and information as it is published.

Our Classes

Finally, our classes are a great opportunity for students to advance in their English in a supportive environment. English Now! has long had a reputation as a trusted, personalized source of high-quality language education. Our small class sizes - maximum six students - and small center size - only eight classrooms - make our atmosphere uniquely home-like. We know our students, and we are creative and imaginative in our approach to the programs that serve them. Please contact us for more information about any of the following offerings:

  • Intensive English Courses at our Bethesda location
  • Conversation Courses at our Bethesda location
  • Private lessons for specific purposes
  • Spring Break and Summer Youth Programs

We can also support your organization's goals with classes at our school site in Bethesda. Alternatively, if your organization is located in the D.C. area, we can teach a group class at your location, taught by our same high quality instructors.

Contact us to learn about how we might collaborate with programs such as the ones described below. We look forward to meeting you and to working with your organization!
