Parent Talk

Parent Talk is an informal conversational group for parents that meets on Fridays, from 10-11:30. Children from newborn to three years old are welcome. Each week we focus on a specific parenting theme, such as feeding, potty training, role of fathers, self-care, discipline, attachment, sleeping, etc. Sometimes articles or book chapters are used to guide the discussion. For example, this winter and spring, Parent Talk is focusing on the book How Eskimos Keep Their Babies Warm: And Other Adventures in Parenting (from Argentina to Tanzania and everywhere in between) In the course of sharing their own perspectives, students learn English parenting-relevant vocabulary and review basic grammar.

Parent Talk is led by Elizabeth Boesen, who has three children and very much enjoys parenting --- even the hard parts, from which she has learned and grown tremendously. Elizabeth says, "though challenging at times, parenting has been the single most rewarding and life-transforming aspect of my life. I want to encourage my Parent Talk students to be curious about their children, to forgive themselves for mistakes, and to take a long-term view on their relationship with their child." Elizabeth is particularly interested in helping parents relax in their child's early years, to attach well to their babies, to remember the bigger picture of their child's life, and to seek to enjoy their children rather than to control them. As for English language, Elizabeth has taught ESOL at Phillips Academy Andover and is a self-described grammar geek, with many well-loved copies of William Safire and Barbara Wallraff books in her collection. She has also completed cross-cultural training programs with the Interchange Institute, a Boston-based research organization focusing on intercultural transitions. Having lived, studied and worked on three continents and in five countries, she loves to learn from her international students about how parenting is experienced in their cultures. She has a background in international youth development and non-profit work, having worked as Special Assistant to the President and Regional Director-Asia of the International Youth Foundation and as a member of the U.S. grantmaking team at the Ashoka Foundation. Elizabeth has an AB in Political Science from the University of Chicago and a Master's in International Relations from the Institute d'Hautes Etudiantes Internationale of the University of Geneva in Switzerland. She resides in Falls Church, Virginia, with her husband and three children.
