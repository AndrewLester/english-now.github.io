Parent Talk

Parent Talk is an informal conversational group for parents that meets on Fridays, from 10-11:30. Children from newborn to three years old are welcome. Each week we focus on a specific parenting theme, such as feeding, potty training, role of fathers, self-care, discipline, attachment, sleeping, etc. Sometimes articles or book chapters are used to guide the discussion. For example, this winter and spring, Parent Talk is focusing on the book How Eskimos Keep Their Babies Warm: And Other Adventures in Parenting (from Argentina to Tanzania and everywhere in between) In the course of sharing their own perspectives, students learn English parenting-relevant vocabulary and review basic grammar.

Parent Talk is led by Elizabeth Boesen. More information about Elizabeth here.
