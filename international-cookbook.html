Welcome to our International Cookbook!

We enjoy sharing a meal together once a month, at our potluck lunches, and we also enjoy sharing great recipes with each other and - on our website - with you. Please try one of these dishes, and we invite additional suggestions about great food from around the world: submit your own recipe here.

Gratin de Courgettes - Zucchini Gratin (Zucchini with Cheese)

  • Preparation time: 30 minutes
  • Cooking time: Five minutes, twice
  • Oven temperature: Broil

Ingredients:

  • 2 organic zucchini per person
  • 2 handfuls of grated Swiss cheese
  • 2 soup spoons of sour cream or crème fraîche (or coconut milk for lactose intolerant)
  • a little nutmeg
  • salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Wash and cut the zucchini into thin slices without peeling.
  2. Cook the sliced zucchini for 5 minutes in boiling water.
  3. Drain very well, using a fork lightly to help extract the water.
  4. Mix the zucchini in a bowl with the sour cream, nutmeg, salt and pepper.
  5. Pour into a baking dish. Add grated cheese on top.
  6. Broil in oven until cheese is melted and crisp.

Contributed by: Sophie from France.

Comments: This recipie is from friends. Bon appétit!

Shredded BBQ Chicken Sandwich

  • Preparation time: 20 minutes total
  • Cooking time: 10 minutes
  • Oven temperature: none

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of shredded cooked chicken (from leftovers or boil chicken in water)
  • 1 cup of apple cider vinegar
  • 1 cup of ketchup
  • 3 tablespoons of packed dark brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of yellow mustard
  • 1 tablespoon of molasses or honey
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of dried crushed red pepper
  • bread (any type you like for sandwiches)

Directions:

  1. For the sauce, mix all ingredients in a mixing bowl and whisk well to dissolve the sugar.
  2. In a saute pan with 1 teaspoon of oil, heat the shredded chicken on medium heat. Add the the desired amount of sauce and cook until heated through and thoroughly covered in the sauce.
  3. Toast your bread and add the BBQ chicken.

Contributed by: Kirstie, from the U.S.

Comments: This recipe comes from "The Nifty Cupcake", with some modifications. Ask Kirstie, our Center Manager, for more information!

Ajiaco - Colombian Chicken and Potato Soup

  • Preparation time: one hour and 30 minutes
  • Cooking time: one hour and ten minutes
  • Oven temperature: none

Ingredients (for 6 - 8 people):

  • 3 chicken breasts, skin removed
  • 12 cups of water
  • 3 ears of fresh corn (each broken into 2 pieces if you want)
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • pepper to taste
  • 2 chicken bouillon cubes
  • garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 tablespoons of chopped cilantro (dried is OK but fresh is better)
  • 3 medium yellow potatoes, peeled and cut into small pieces
  • 3 medium red potatoes, peeled and cut into small pieces
  • 1/3 cup of guascas, a leafy herb from Colombia (this herb is very important for the taste of this recipe and it isn't basil or parsley - He He!!)
  • 1 cup of heavy cream (or a big spoon of milk cream for each serving if someone can not have cream)
  • avocados to taste, when serving

Directions:

  1. In a large pot, place the chicken, corn, chicken bouillon, cilantro, onions, garlic, salt and pepper.
  2. Add the water and bring to a boil.
  3. Then reduce the heat to medium and cook for about 35 to 40 minutes, until chicken is cooked and tender.
  4. Remove the chicken and cut meat into small pieces and return pieces to the pot.
  5. Add the guasca and cook for 30 more minutes. Taste, and add more salt if needed.
  6. Serve the Ajiaco hot with avocado and heavy cream on the side. Be ready to enjoy it!!

Contributed by: Luz from Columbia

Comments: This is a typical Columbian recipe. Enjoy!

Chocotorta - Choco-cake

  • Preparation time: 3 hours
  • Cooking time: none
  • Oven temperature: none

Ingredients:

  • Can (15oz) of Dulce de Leche
  • 8 oz package of Cream cheese
  • 12 oz of coffee
  • A package of chocolate cookies

Directions:

  1. First put the coffee in a bowl.
  2. Put several cookies in the coffee.
  3. Place the cookies one by one into an aluminum pan to make one layer.
  4. Mix the cream cheese and dulce de leche in a bowl.
  5. Cover the layer of cookies with the cream cheese and dulce de leche mixture.
  6. Put several more cookies into the coffee and then put another layer of them one by one into the pan.
  7. Cover this layer with the cream cheese and dulce de leche.
  8. Make another layer of cookies in the pan, and then do it again, to fill your pan.
  9. Finally, put the Chocotorta in the fridge for 3 hours, and then you can enjoy this dessert!

Contributed by: Malena from Argentina

Bignés di San Giuseppe - St. Joseph Day's Cream Puffs

  • Preparation time: 1 hour
  • Cooking time: 40 minutes
  • Oven temperature: none
  • Ingredients:

For the bignés (dough):

  • 150 gr. of flour (about 1 cup, all-purpose)
  • 80 gr. of butter (about 1/3 cup)
  • 200 gr. water (about 1 1/4 cups)
  • 4 eggs
  • 60 gr. of sugar ( about 1/4 cup)
  • a pinch of salt

For the crema pasticcera (pastry cream):

  • 500 ml. of milk (1 pint)
  • 150 gr. of sugar (about 3/4 cup)
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 1/2 teaspoon of vanilla extract or vanilla bean
  • 25 gr. of cornstarch (about 4 tablespoons)

For frying:

  • vVgetable oil or canola oil mixed with olive oil, enough to cover the dough balls

To finish:

  • Powdered sugar

Directions:

Crema pasticcera (pastry cream):

  • Put the milk and the vanilla in a saucepan and simmer on low heat.
  • In a bowl, beat the egg yolks and the sugar with an eggbeater or a whisk.
  • Add the cornstarch to the yolks, stirring to dissolve.
  • Pour the milk into the bowl slowly, stirring constantly (first remove the vanilla bean from the milk if that was used).
  • Pour this mixture into a small saucepan and cook the cream, stirring constantly so that no lumps are created.
  • Simmer, stirring, until the cream thickens.
  • Remove the cream from the heat and pour it into a mixing bowl and let it cool, covered with plastic wrap. (the plastic should be touching the cream, to prevent a crust.)

For the bignés:

  • Put the water, the butter, the sugar, and the salt in a saucepan and put the pan on a burner on top of the stove, mixing the ingredients with a wooden spoon while heating to a simmer.
  • Next, take the saucepan off the heat, add the flour and mix well until the flour is well incorporated.
  • Put the pan back on the heat and stir until the mixture breaks away from the sides of the pan and sticks together to form a ball. This should be quick and take only a few seconds.
  • Next, turn off the burner, remove the pan, and cool the mixture a bit.
  • Add the eggs one at a time, mixing well with a wooden spoon until each is well incorporated into the dough.
  • Cool the dough completely. It will be quite sticky and soft.
  • Put the oil into another saucepan and heat it to a temperature of 160 degrees C (320 degrees F).
  • To fry the bignés, take two spoons and with one of them, pick up a little of the dough; then pass the dough from one spoon to another, forming a little dough ball (it's impossible to make the ball perfectly round).
  • As you make each dough ball, out it into the hot oil to fry.
  • Move the pan gently and the balls will rotate easily. When they are puffy, raise the temperature to 170 degrees C (338 degrees F) and continue frying until they are golden brown.
  • Remove the bignés from the oil with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
  • Put the pastry cream into a pastry bag with a smooth pastry tip and pierce gently the center of each bigné.
  • Squeeze a small amount of cream into each bigné.
  • Sprinkle powdered sugar onto the bignés.
  • Eat immediately to enjoy them hot and crispy, or can store them in the refrigerator, covered, for 2 days at the most.

NOTE: It is possible to cook the bignés in the oven, for a lighter version (180 degrees C/350 degrees F) for about 20 - 30 minutes, or until golden brown.

Contributed by: Simonetta from Italy

Comments: Typically made for St. Joseph's Day

Pumpkin Mac and Cheese

  • Preparation time: 1 hour
  • Cooking time: Bake for 30 minutes
  • Oven temperature: 350 degrees F

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups dried elbow macaroni
  • 2 tablespoon butter
  • 2 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 cup whipping cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 4 oz. Fontina cheese, shredded (1 cup)
  • 1 15.oz. can pumpkin or fresh pumpkin
  • 1 tablespoon snipped fresh sage or 1/2 teaspoon dried leaf sage, crushed
  • 1/2 cup soft bread crumbs
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/3 cup chopped walnuts - 1 tab. olive oil

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. Cook pasta in a large pot following package directions. Drain cooked pasta, then return to pot.
  2. For cheese sauce, in a medium saucepan melt butter over medium heat. Stir in flour, salt and pepper. Add whipping cream and milk all at once. Cook and stir over medium heat until slightly thickened and bubbly. Stir in cheese, pumpkin, and sage until cheese is melted. Stir cheese sauce into pasta to coat. Transfer macaroni and cheese to an ungreased 2-quart pan.
  3. In a small bowl combine bread crumbs, Parmesan, walnuts and oil; sprinkle over pasta. Bake, uncovered for 30 minutes or until bubbly and top is golden. Let stand 10 minutes before serving.

Contributed by: Laurel from the USA

Empanada de Pollo - Chicken Pie

  • Preparation time: 2-2 1/2 hours
  • Cooking time: 45 minutes
  • Oven temperature: 350 degrees F

Ingredients:

  • Serves 8 to 10 people For the Pastry/Pie Crust:
  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 4 ounces (8 tablespoons) lard, chilled and cut into 1/2-inch pieces
  • 2 ounces (4 tablespoons) unsalted butter, chilled and cut into 1/2
  • inch pieces
  • 2 egg yolks and 1 whole egg
  • 4 tablespoons dry white wine For the Filling:
  • 2 2 1/2-pound fryers, cut into serving pieces
  • Lemon juice
  • Salt, freshly ground pepper
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 medium onions, finely chopped
  • 2 green peppers, finely chopped
  • 1 pound tomatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 1 teaspoon Spanish (hot) paprika
  • Bay leaf
  • 2 tablespoons chopped, pitted olives
  • 2 teaspoons capers
  • 2 canned pimientos, chopped
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, sliced

Directions:

For the Pastry/Pie Crust:

  • Sift the flour, baking powder and sugar into a large bowl.
  • Rub the fat into the flour with tips of the fingers until the mixture resembles coarse meal.
  • Beat the egg yolks and the egg lightly with 2 tablespoons of the wine and blend into the fat-flour mixture.
  • Knead quickly into a ball. If the dough crumbles, sprinkle up to two tablespoons more of wine over it until it holds together.
  • Shape into 2 balls, one slightly larger than the other, wrap in wax paper and refrigerate until ready to use. For the Filling:
  • Pat the chicken pieces dry, rub with lemon juice and season to taste with salt and pepper.
  • Heat the oil in a large, heavy frying pan and sauté the chicken pieces until golden. Lift out and set aside.
  • Add the onions and green peppers to the frying pan and sauté until the onion is tender but not browned.
  • Add the tomatoes, paprika and bay leaf. Season to taste with salt and pepper, stir to mix well, cover and simmer gently for 15 minutes.
  • Return the chicken pieces to the pan, cover and simmer for about 30 minutes, or until chicken can be boned easily. Do not overcook.
  • Allow to cool, bone the chicken, and cut the meat into bite-sized pieces.
  • At this point, check if the sauce is at all watery and if necessary reduce the sauce over brisk heat for a few minutes. The sauce should be quite thick.
  • Remove and discard the bay leaf. Add the olives, capers, pimientos and chicken and mix gently. Set aside. To put the Empanada together:
  • Remove the pastry from the refrigerator and roll out the larger ball to fit a 2 to 2 1/2-inch deep by 7 by 12-inch rectangular pyrex or other baking dish.
  • Line the pie dish with the dough, allowing the pastry to overlap the edges of the dish.
  • Fill the shell with the chicken and sauce mixture. Arrange the egg slices on top.
  • Roll out the smaller ball of pastry for the top crust of the empanada.
  • Trim, then moisten the edges of the lower crust with water, cover with the top crust, and seal well. Pinch with fingers to make a decorative border or mark with a fork.
  • Cut three or four slits in the top to allow the steam to escape during cooking.
  • Bake in a 350-degree oven for 45 minutes, or until the crust is golden.
  • Serve hot or cold.

Contributed by: Cristina from USA/Cuba

Comments: This recipe came from Cristina's mother, Nieves Rendueles, from an old Cuban recipe (Oriente Province) which was given to Nieves by her mother-in-law

"Aji de Gallina" - Peruvian Spicy Creamed Chicken

  • Preparation time: n/a
  • Cooking time: n/a
  • Oven temperature: n/a

Ingredients:

  • 1 and 1/2 pounds of chicken breast
  • 4 cups of chicken stock
  • 1/4 cup of vegetable oil
  • 5 yellow aji peppers
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 1 large onion (finely chopped)
  • 3 tablespoons of grated Parmesan cheese
  • 4 slices of white bread
  • 3/4 cup of evaporated milk
  • 4 yellow potatoes
  • 2 hard boiled eggs

Directions:

  1. Cook the yellow potatoes, let them cool.
  2. Peel them and cut into quarters.
  3. Cook the chick breast in a pot with the chicken stock.
  4. Reserve 2 cups of strained broth.
  5. Remove stems and seeds from the peppers.
  6. In a blender, process peppers with the vegetable oil until smooth.
  7. Sauté the garlic and onions with the puréed peppers and oil.
  8. Shred the cooked chicken into bite-sized pieces.
  9. In a blender, process the strained broth with the bread.
  10. Add # 8 to # 6.
  11. Add 1 and 1/2 cups of reserved chicken stock.
  12. Stir in the chicken.
  13. Add evaporated milk and Parmesan cheese.
  14. Serve over rice, garnished with the potatoes and slices of hard boiled eggs.

Contributed by: Jessica from Peru

Isombe - Cassava Leaf

  • Preparation time: 60 minutes
  • Cooking time: 60 minutes
  • Oven temperature: none

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 kg.of cassava leaf
  • 1/4 kg. of cut fresh celery
  • 2 green peppers cut in medium pieces
  • 4 onions, cut in medium pieces
  • 4 tomatoes, cut in medium pieces
  • 2 1/2 cups of tomato sauce
  • 1/2 kg. of small fish (lake fish like "Ndagala")
  • salt, to taste
  • 2 cups of olive oil
  • 4 whole garlic, peeled
  • 1/2 pan of water

Directions:

  1. Cook the cassava leaf in the half pan of water until the water boils.
  2. Put all the other ingredients in the pan except for the olive oil and the tomato sauce.
  3. Cook for one hour on medium heat.
  4. Add the 2 cups of olive oil and the 2 and ½ cups of tomato sauce to the pan.
  5. Mix all ingredients well on low heat.
  6. Your "Isombe" is now ready to eat!

Contributed by: Antoinette from Burundi

Okonomi-yaki ("As-you-like-it" Pancake)

  • Preparation time: 20 minutes
  • Cooking time: 20 minutes
  • Oven temperature: n/a

Ingredients:

  • For 4 servings:
  • 3 and 1/2 oz. of pork, thinly sliced
  • 3 and 1/2 oz. of shrimp (body only); remove heads, tails and veins (or you can use cleaned frozen shrimp, thawed)
  • 7 oz. of chopped cabbage
  • 6 tablespoons of chopped green onion
  • 2 cups of flour
  • 1 and 1/4 cups of water
  • 1 teaspoon of dashi powder
  • 4 to 6 eggs
  • vegetable oil

For the topping:

  • okonomi-yaki sauce
  • bonito powder
  • green seaweed flakes
  • mayonnaise

Note: If you want to make this dish vegetarian, use only the vegetables.

Directions:

  1. Cut pork into 3-inch pieces, chop cabbage and green onions coarsely.
  2. Mix flour, water and dashi-powder in a bowl. Add beaten eggs, stirring lightly.
  3. Add shrimp, cabbage and green onions to the same bowl, and mix well.
  4. Set this mixture aside.
  5. Cook the meat in a lightly oiled frying pan.
  6. Spoon the mixture from the bowl into the frying pan over the meat, making 4 pancakes.
  7. Cook for a few minutes.
  8. When the surface of the pancakes bubbles, turn them over and cook for 5 to 7 minutes.
  9. Flip them over again and spread okonomi-yaki sauce on top of the pancakes.
  10. Drizzle mayonnaise on top.
  11. Finally, sprinkle bonito powder and green-seaweed flakes on top of each pancake.

Contributed by: Keiko from Japan

Comments: This is a recipe from Western Japan.
